8. **Child-test every chapter** — an attentive child who has read only the prior chapters can follow this one end to end.
9. **Sign-off: Scotland.**

**One addition to the law, forced by a question raised during planning and binding from here on:**

10. **The word "forced," handled honestly.** The book uses "forced" as the corpus does, but because *forcing* is also an unrelated technical term in set theory (Cohen's method) and because "forced" is **not** a synonym for "proved," the book must, once and early (in the methodology material, see Phase 3), explain in plain words exactly what "forced" means here: *the one move, run from the One in the permitted language, produces this and only this, with nothing fed in from outside — and where there is a measured value to check against, it matches.* The book must never let "forced" drift into claiming deductive proof of physical necessity, and must never call a result "proved" or "a theorem" of the framework's own physics. The distinction is a strength, stated plainly, not a weakness to hide.

## 4. The proofreading gate (runs after EVERY pass)

A pass is not complete until its manuscript passes all applicable checks. The gate is the book's analogue of the corpus's no-apparatus gate — it fails loudly and the work does not advance until it is green.

**A — No mathematics on the page.** Mechanical scan for stray symbols, equations, digits-standing-for-ideas, notation. Any hit is fixed before the gate passes. (Genuine spoken numbers — "three dimensions", "one part in eight" — are allowed; bare formulae are not.)

**B — Dependency integrity.** No chapter uses a term or idea before the chapter that builds it. Checked against the spine order.

**C — Corpus-faithfulness.** Every substantive claim in the pass maps to a forced result; no claim says more than the corpus forces. Where the corpus is careful (a correspondence the author draws vs. the system forcing), the book is careful in plain words. Special sub-check: **"forced" is never used as "proved"**, and nothing is called a theorem of the framework's own physics.

**D — Overclaim / wellbeing review** (heightened for Part Fourteen). The non-ordinary-experience, mind, machine-consciousness, placebo, and UAP material is checked to ensure it states the structural envelope without sensationalism, never asserts an empirical claim the corpus does not force, and never drifts into anything that could mislead a vulnerable reader. The care the technical law demands around mind and the non-ordinary is enforced in the prose.

**E — Voice and child-test.** Single authorial voice, spoken register, warmth-by-exactness; an attentive newcomer can follow the pass's chapters end to end with only the prior chapters.

**F — Record-faithfulness** (Pass 6 only, the "How It Was Made" chapter). Every claim about the build's history is checked against the actual transcripts; no reconstructed order, no fabricated detail, no attributed words the record does not carry.

**G — Publishable polish.** Spelling, grammar, punctuation, paragraph flow, no repetition, no dangling references. (The author's own spelling is never a measure of the thinking — but the finished manuscript is copy-clean for publication, which is a courtesy to the reader, not a concession to the priesthood.)

Each gate's findings are logged; failures are fixed in place before advancing. This is what "proofread after every phase to ensure it's publishable as it's being written" means, made into a procedure.
