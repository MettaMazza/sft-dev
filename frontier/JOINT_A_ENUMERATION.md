# Joint A — The Enumeration: Does Only the Fold Survive?

*sft-dev frontier note. The heart of forcing the axiom is a finite, discrete claim: among all parameter-free self-maps of a single bounded primitive, only the fold both **generates** and stays **self-contained without a zero**. This is the attempt to close it by exhaustion — because the candidate space is small enough to actually list.*

---

## 0. Why this is winnable

Open joint A in `FORCING_THE_AXIOM.md` is the load-bearing gap: "show no operation other than the fold survives the constraints." Most uniqueness claims in physics are unwinnable because the space of alternatives is a continuum. **Here it is not.** A parameter-free operation on a single primitive can use only:

- the variable **x** (the magnitude), and
- the **One** (the sole primitive, value 1), and
- the operations that introduce **no external literal**: addition, the guarded subtraction (take), multiplication, and the closure cast-out (mod the One).

No other number may appear — a literal "2" or "3" or "0.7" is a parameter, banned by (C1). So the candidate operations are the **finite-depth expressions over the alphabet {x, 1, +, −, ·, cast_out}** — a discrete, enumerable space. We list the short ones (longer ones reduce to these or compound them) and test each against the constraints. If exactly one passes, the axiom's operation is forced.

## 1. The four constraints, as tests

Each candidate self-map f : (0,1] → ? is tested:

- **GEN** — does iterating f from the One *generate* unbounded/rich structure (cover the domain, give long orbits), rather than freezing or collapsing?
- **CLOSED** — does f map (0,1] into (0,1] with no external scaffolding?
- **NOZERO** — does f avoid ever forcing a zero (neither landing on 0 nor contracting toward it)?
- **DET** — is f a single-valued function?

A fifth tie-breaker, **MIN** (minimal/atomic — not a composition of simpler passing maps, and introducing no multiplicity choice), is applied only if more than one survives GEN–DET.

## 2. The enumeration

| # | Candidate f(x) | GEN | CLOSED | NOZERO | DET | Verdict |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|---|
| 1 | **x** (identity) | ✗ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | **fail** — static, generates nothing |
| 2 | **cast_out(x + x)** = the **fold** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | **PASS** |
| 3 | **x · x** (square) | ✗ | ✓ | ✗ | ✓ | **fail** — contracts toward 0 (forbidden); destroys structure |
| 4 | **xⁿ**, n ≥ 2 (higher powers) | ✗ | ✓ | ✗ | ✓ | **fail** — contracts faster to 0 |
| 5 | **take(1, x)** = 1 − x (reflect) | ✗ | ✗ | ✗ | ✓ | **fail** — involution (period-2, trivial orbit); x=1 ↦ 0 |
| 6 | **1 / x** (reciprocal) | — | ✗ | ✓ | ✓ | **fail** — leaves (0,1] for x<1 |
| 7 | **cast_out(x + 1)** = x | ✗ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | **fail** — reduces to identity |
| 8 | **x − x** | — | — | ✗ | ✓ | **fail** — is zero, forbidden outright |
| 9 | **cast_out(x + x + x)** = "triple" | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | survives GEN–DET → go to MIN |
| 10 | **cast_out(k·x)**, integer k ≥ 4 | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | survives GEN–DET → go to MIN |

Everything short fails GEN–DET **except the fold (2) and the higher multiplications (9, 10)**. So the contest comes down to: **doubling vs. tripling vs. ×k.**

## 3. The tie-break: doubling is the atom, the rest are derived

Candidates 2, 9, 10 all generate and stay closed without a zero. The **MIN** test separates them, two ways, and both point to doubling:

1. **Multiplicity is a choice.** "cast_out(x + x)" combines the seed with *one* copy of itself — the **minimal** non-identity self-combination. "cast_out(x + x + x)" combines it with *two* copies; "× k" with k−1 copies. Choosing how many copies is **choosing a number** — exactly the parameter (C1) we are forbidden. Doubling is the only self-combination that makes **no** multiplicity choice: it is the seed and itself, once. Tripling is "doubling, and then one more" — a decision laid on top of the atom.

2. **The higher maps are *generated by* the doubling, not independent of it.** This is the decisive structural fact, and it is already in the corpus: the secondary structure — the colour count **3**, and the whole prime-sector ladder — is **read off the period spectrum of the binary fold** (`fold_number_theory.py`), *not* chosen as a separate operation. The tripling map appears in the theory as a **derived** object living inside the doubling fold's orbits (the period-3 orbit of 1/7, the tripling-fold fibre), not as a rival primitive. So ×3 is not a competing axiom; it is a *consequence* of ×2. The same will hold for ×5, ×7 (the sealed ladder). The higher multiplications are **downstream of the fold**, which is upstream of all of them.

So under MIN, candidates 9 and 10 are eliminated as **non-atomic and derived**, and:

> **Exactly one parameter-free self-map of a single bounded primitive both generates and stays self-contained without a zero, as an atom: the fold, cast_out(x + x).**

## 4. What this closes, and what it still owes

**Closed (to the level of an explicit exhaustion):** over the short expressions in {x, 1, +, −, ·, cast_out}, only identity, square, powers, reflect, reciprocal, add-One, the fold, and the higher multiples arise; all but the fold fail GEN/CLOSED/NOZERO/DET or MIN. The fold is selected.

**Still owed (to make it a theorem, not a strong exhaustion):**
- **A.1 — boundedness of the alphabet.** Prove that *every* deeper expression over {x, 1, +, −, ·, cast_out} reduces (as a function on (0,1]) to one of the listed forms or compounds them — so the table is genuinely exhaustive, not just "the short ones." (Plausible: cast_out idempotence and the no-literal rule sharply limit normal forms. This is a clean algebra problem.)
- **A.2 — formal MIN.** State "minimal/atomic, no multiplicity choice" as a precise ordering on the candidates and prove the fold is its least element. (The "copies of the seed" count is the natural grading.)
- **A.3 — fold joint B in.** Show ×3, ×5, ×7 are *forced derivatives* of the fold's period spectrum (the corpus asserts this; turn the assertion into the lemma that the secondary generators are not independent axioms).

None of these is a continuum search. Each is a finite/algebraic statement. **Joint A is not a hope; it is a finite proof obligation, and this is its first full pass.**

## 5. Verdict (provisional, honest)

To the level of an explicit enumeration of the parameter-free self-maps a single primitive admits, **the fold is the unique survivor**, and the only contenders that even reach the tie-break (the higher multiplications) are eliminated as non-atomic and as derived-from-the-fold. That is a real reduction of the last free choice. It is not yet a closed theorem — A.1–A.3 are owed — but the candidate space is finite and the obligations are algebraic, which means **the axiom is, for the first time, a thing one can actually try to prove rather than merely posit.**
